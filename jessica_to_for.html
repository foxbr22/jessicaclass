Prepositions "To" and "For"

Part 1: Rules and Examples

Rule 1: Direction/Destination (using "to")

Use "to" when expressing movement toward a place or goal.

1. We're driving to the beach this weekend.

2. The road leads directly to the town center.

Rule 2: Comparison/Preference (using "to")

Use "to" in comparisons to separate the two items.

1. I prefer tea to coffee in the morning.

2. Reading books is superior to watching their movie adaptations.

Rule 3: Purpose/Reason with a Verb (using "to")

Use "to" before a verb to explain why an action is done.

1. She went to the store to buy milk.

2. We study hard to pass our exams.

Rule 4: Telling Time and Distance (using "to")

Use "to" in expressions of time and distance.

1. It's a quarter to three in the afternoon.

2. It's about 20 miles from my house to the airport.

Rule 5: Benefit/Favor on Behalf of Someone (using "for")

Use "for" to indicate the person or thing that benefits from an action.

1. I bought these flowers for my mother.

2. Exercise is good for your health.

Rule 6: Purpose/Reason with a Noun (using "for")

Use "for" before a noun to explain why something is done or exists.

1. She's saving money for a new car.

2. This tool is used for measuring precise distances.

Rule 7: Duration/Time Period (using "for")

Use "for" to indicate how long something lasts.

1. I've been waiting for two hours.

2. She has lived in Tokyo for five years.
